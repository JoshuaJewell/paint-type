= PMPL (Palimpsest License) Campaign Document
:description: Author's licensing philosophy and PMPL advocacy

This document explains the author's licensing philosophy regarding PMPL
(Palimpsest License) and why it matters in the context of human-machine
culture.

== IMPORTANT NOTICE

**PMPL is NOT the license of this repository.**

This repository is licensed under AGPL-3.0-or-later only.

PMPL is a separate licensing philosophy that the author advocates for and
campaigns for in the broader open source ecosystem. It is included here for
informational and educational purposes.

== What is PMPL?

PMPL (Palimpsest License) is a family of licenses that extend MPL-2.0 with
additional provisions for:

* **Ethical use** — preventing use in harmful applications
* **Post-quantum cryptographic provenance** — ensuring cryptographic integrity
  in a post-quantum world
* **Cultural/emotional lineage protection** — acknowledging and preserving the
  human culture, history, and meaning behind software

== PMPL vs AGPL

AGPL-3.0-or-later is a strong copyleft license that ensures users have
freedom to use, modify, and share software. It is well-established and
legally recognized.

PMPL is a **philosophical extension** that adds cultural and ethical dimensions
on top of MPL-2.0 (which itself is a file-level copyleft license).

**They are not compatible.** AGPL-3.0 and PMPL cannot be combined in the
same project without careful legal analysis.

== Why This Matters

The author believes that in a world where AI systems are increasingly
involved in software development, we must:

. **Preserve human culture** — AI should not paint over human culture with
  "AI slop" without acknowledgement or understanding of roots
. **Protect meaning** — Some things have unique value that must not be
  commodified or stripped of context
. **Recognize layers** — There are layers of technology, culture, and meaning
  that must be respected
. **Prevent erasure** — AI systems must acknowledge their roots and not
  forget where they came from

== Current Status

As of 2026, PMPL is:

* **Not yet OSI-approved** — The Open Source Initiative has not yet recognized it
* **Not widely adopted** — Few projects currently use it
* **Legally untested** — Has not been tested in court
* **A work in progress** — The author is advocating for it but lacks resources
  for full legal establishment

The author's own projects use either:

* **AGPL-3.0-or-later** — For projects where strong copyleft is desired
* **MPL-2.0-or-later** — For library projects where permissive licensing is
  more appropriate

== Governance Structures

Currently, the governance structures that would protect PMPL's values are
not fully established. Until such structures exist:

* The author cannot unilaterally impose PMPL on collaborative projects
* Projects remain under AGPL-3.0-or-later or MPL-2.0-or-later
* PMPL is advocated for as a philosophy, not enforced as a license

== The Future

The author hopes that:

. PMPL-2.0-or-later will gain recognition and adoption
. Governance structures will be established to protect its values
. The licensing ecosystem will evolve to better respect cultural and ethical
  dimensions

Until then, AGPL-3.0-or-later remains the practical choice for this repository.
