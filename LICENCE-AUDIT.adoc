== Detailed Analysis

=== Option A: Full AGPL-3.0-or-later Migration

**Pros:**
- Strong copyleft (your requirement)
- Well-established in FOSS community
- Clear licensing terms

**Cons:**
- Incompatible with current PMPL-1.0-or-later
- Would require relicensing all contributions
- May conflict with PMPL's quantum-ready provisions
- Loses PMPL's unique features (emotional lineage, etc.)

**Effort:** High - requires contributor approval

=== Option B: Dual License (AGPL-3.0-or-later OR PMPL-1.0-or-later)

**Pros:**
- Gives users choice
- Maintains quantum-ready features
- Strong copyleft available

**Cons:**
- Complex for users to understand
- Contribution agreement complications
- May not satisfy "must be AGPL-3.0-or-later" requirement

**Effort:** Medium

=== Option C: Keep PMPL-1.0-or-later

**Pros:**
- Already quantum-ready
- Already in use
- No disruption
- Maintains unique features

**Cons:**
- Does not meet your stated requirement of AGPL-3.0-or-later
- PMPL not yet OSI-approved (fallback to MPL-2.0 works)

**Effort:** None

=== Option D: AGPL-3.0-or-later with PMPL-1.0-or-later Fallback (RECOMMENDED)

**Pros:**
- Meets your AGPL-3.0-or-later requirement
- Maintains quantum-ready features via PMPL fallback
- Clear license hierarchy
- Respects PMPL's existing contributions

**Cons:**
- Slightly complex
- Requires contributor agreement updates

**Implementation:**
[source,text]
----
SPDX-License-Identifier: AGPL-3.0-or-later
Fallback-License: PMPL-1.0-or-later

This work is primarily licensed under AGPL-3.0-or-later.
Where AGPL-3.0-or-later is not accepted, PMPL-1.0-or-later applies
(which extends MPL-2.0 with quantum-ready provisions).
----

**Effort:** Medium
